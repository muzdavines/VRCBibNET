The Tabernacle Among Us
Exodus 25–40
 John 1:14
Intro
Last time we stood at Sinai.
God descended in fire.  The law was given.
Israel knows GOD IS HOLY.
?¬  who God is. ?= How can this God dwell among this people?
Exodus 25–40 answers that.
¬ theory. In structure, sacrifice, presence.
1. God Dwells Among His People
READ: Exodus 25:1–9
“That I May Dwell Among Them”
Exegesis
“Let them make me a sanctuary, that I may dwell in their midst.”
That is the controlling line.
God initiates the offering,  specifies the materials  gives the pattern.
“Exactly as I show you.”
¬ Israel designing worship, God prescribing access.
Even the materials preach: GOLD , glory and kingship.
LINEN, purity.
ACACIA WOOD, endurance in the wilderness.
ordered, deliberate, revealed.
Types and Shadows
God dwelling in a tent among His people is not the end.
It is a shadow, It points forward to a greater dwelling.
Not fabric and poles, Flesh.
Specifies Worship:
“The true worshipers will worship the Father in spirit and truth.” (John 4:23)
Word (preaching, reading Scripture), Prayer, Singing (psalms, hymns, spiritual songs), Ordinances (baptism, the Lord’s Supper)

¬ our preference
¬ what works, draws a crowd, feel meaningful

But what has God said.

WORSHIP / GOSPEL
EXODUS~ No Blood, No access.
TODAY ~ No Christ, No Worship
Sing, pray, feel - No Christ - outside the Veil.
Metaphor
This is not a ladders / tower to heaven.
This is God pitching His tent in the middle of a broken camp.
Quote
Calvin:
 “God accommodates Himself to our capacity.”
Application
We instinctively think we approach God on our terms.
Effort. Sincerity. preference.
But the tabernacle says:
If God is to dwell with you, it will be HIS WAY OR NOT AT ALL.

2. The Way In Is Not Casual
READ: Exodus 25:21–22, 26:33
Exegesis
“There I will meet with you.”
? walk straight in?
You move through a structure:
 . Ark, presence above the mercy seat.
.  Altar, blood first.
. Veil, separation.
. Priest, mediated access.

Every layer restricts. steps emphasise holiness. Designed PATH
¬ Not random detail.
Types and Shadows
Each element points forward:
 Mercy seat -> propitiation - wrath appeased 
 Sacrifice -> Christ’s death
 Veil -> access not yet open
 Priest -> Christ’s mediation
The whole system is a shadow of a better way.
Metaphor
It is a corridor where every step forward is paid for in blood.
Quote
John Owen: “There is no access unto God but by blood.”
Application
We want nearness without cost.
We want God without sacrifice.
The tabernacle says: If God is near, something has died.

3. Eden Remembered, Not Restored
Exodus 25:31-40, 26:31-35
Exegesis
Notice patterns:
. Cherubim woven into the veil.
. Lampstand shaped like a tree.
. Gold, light, life imagery.
Deep theology not just decoration - It echoes Eden.
But there is still a veil. Still guarded access.
proximity, not restoration.
Types and Shadows
tabernacle = partial return to Eden.
¬ complete it. Pointing foreward.
Metaphor
It is Eden behind curtains. Visible, but not accessible.
Quote
Augustine:  “Our hearts are restless until they rest in You.”
Application
Religion can bring you near to sacred things. can’t bring you home.
It leaves you close, but still outside.

4. The Golden Calf
READ: Exodus 32:1–4
Exegesis
While God is giving instructions for His dwelling…
The people are making an idol. “Make us gods.”
The heart.
God reveals Himself. Man replaces Him.
Judgment follows, Moses intercedes.
The covenant continues, not because of Israel’s faithfulness, but because of God’s mercy.
Types and Shadows
Moses stands as mediator, type not final
He points forward to a greater mediator who will not merely plead, but atone.
Metaphor
The tabernacle is built on the far side of betrayal.
Quote
Spurgeon: “God’s mercy shines brightest against the darkest sin.”
Application
Left to ourselves, we do not build tabernacles, We build idols.
The problem is not lack of instruction.It is a corrupted heart.
We need mercy, not just guidance.

5. The Glory Fills the Tabernacle
READ: Exodus 40:34–35
Exegesis
“The glory of the LORD filled the tabernacle.”
God has come near, His presence is real.
But then:“Moses was not able to enter.”
Even the mediator cannot fully access the presence.
The tension remains.
God is with them. But they are not fully with Him.
Types and Shadows
This is the limitation of the Old Covenant.
Real presence, Restricted access.
It anticipates a greater opening.
Metaphor The glory is like fire in a house.
Life-giving, but not casually approached.
Quote
R.C. Sproul:  “The holiness of God is not safe.”
Application
Can’t treat God casually.
Familiarity without reverence leads to destruction.
Conclusion – Fulfilment in Christ
READ: John 1:14
Exegesis
“The Word became flesh and dwelt among us.”
He tabernacled, ¬ symbolically, Literally.
God’s presence is now personal.
Types and Shadows
Everything converges in Christ:
He is the tabernacle, the priest, the sacrifice, mercy seat.
The tent becomes a person.
Quote
Athanasius:“He became what we are, that He might make us what He is.”
He became what we are, not to make us God, but to bring us into what He gives by grace, His life, His righteousness, and His sonship.
Application
The question is ¬ where God dwells.
The question is whether you have come to Him.
Final Application and Gospel
READ: Hebrews 10:19–21
19 Therefore, brothers,[a] since we have confidence to enter the holy places by the blood of Jesus, 20 by the new and living way that he opened for us through the curtain, that is, through his flesh, 21 and since we have a great priest over the house of God, 22 let us draw near with a true heart in full assurance of faith, with our hearts sprinkled clean from an evil conscience and our bodies washed with pure water.
Exegesis
“We have confidence to enter… by the blood of Jesus.”
What Exodus could not fully accomplish…Christ completes.
Access is now open.
Types and Shadows
The veil is no longer a barrier. In Christ, it is torn.
Metaphor
What was once a guarded doorway is now an open entrance.
Quote
John Calvin: “Christ is the only door by which we enter into the kingdom of God.”
Application
You are not invited to stand near.
You are invited to come in.
Not by effort, Not by religion, By blood.
God does not dwell with us because we built something worthy.
 He dwells with us because Christ made a way.
