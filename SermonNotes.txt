A Promise to Bless All Nations
Genesis 12:1–9
Introduction
Babel vs Abraham
Genesis 11 ended with humanity saying: “Let us make a name for ourselves.”
Issac - Gen 22
Humanity united around pride.
Humanity attempting to climber upward toward heaven. {Dark night Rises}
Genesis 12 begins with something completely different.
God speaks to one man and says: “I will make your name great.”
Babel shows humanity trying to secure its own glory.
Genesis 12 begins God’s plan to give blessing through promise.
Someone contrasted:“Babel builds a tower.  God begins redemption with a promise.”
This moment marks one of the greatest turning points in Scripture.
After the scattering of the nations at Babel, God begins a plan to bless those nations.
“Babel shows humanity trying to make a name for itself. Genesis 12 begins with God saying to Abram, ‘I will make your name great.’”

1. The Call of God
Genesis 12:1 - pagan
“Now the Lord said to Abram, “Go from your country and your kindred and your father's house to the land that I will show you.”
~~ EXEgesis ~~
“Go from your country and your kindred and your father’s house to the land that I will show you.”
Abram is to leave 3 things, defined security in the ancient world:
• his land
 • his extended family
 • his inheritance
God does not give Abram a map. {4M}
He gives him a promise and a direction.
Hebrews later reflects:
“By faith Abraham obeyed when he was called… and he went out, not knowing where he was going.” (Hebrews 11:8)
Faith doesn’t begin with certainty about the future but with trust in the God who speaks.
Mark 1: 16 Passing alongside the Sea of Galilee, he saw Simon and Andrew the brother of Simon casting a net into the sea, for they were fishermen. 17 And Jesus said to them, “Follow me, and I will make you become fishers of men.”[f] 18 And immediately they left their nets and followed him. 19 And going on a little farther, he saw James the son of Zebedee and John his brother, who were in their boat mending the nets. 20 And immediately he called them, and they left their father Zebedee in the boat with the hired servants and followed him.
~~ Quote ~~
John Calvin: “Abraham’s faith rested not in knowing the end of the journey but in trusting the One who commanded the journey.”
Faith rests in God’s character, not in visible outcomes.
Illustration – The Compass in the Fog
Imagine standing on a mountain path covered in thick fog. {4m in the rain}
You cannot see the valley below.
 You cannot see the destination.
All you have is a compass.
You take the next step because you trust the compass.
That is Abraham in Genesis 12.
God didn’t give him a map.
God gave him a direction.
~~ APP ~~
Faith often means stepping forward when the destination is still hidden.
One preacher put it this way: “Faith walks where sight has not yet travelled.”
Abraham obeyed before he understood the whole plan.
The Christian life begins the same way.

2. The Promise of Blessing
Genesis 12:2–3
2 And I will make of you a great nation, and I will bless you and make your name great, so that you will be a blessing. 3 I will bless those who bless you, and him who dishonors you I will curse, and in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed.”
~~ EXEgesis ~~
God now unfolds the promise:
“I will make of you a great nation.”
 “I will bless you.”
 “I will make your name great.”
 “You will be a blessing.”
Then astonishing statement:
“All the families of the earth shall be blessed.”
Contrast with Babel.
At Babel humanity said: “Let us make a name for ourselves.”
Here God says: “I will make your name great.”
Humanity tried to manufacture a name.
God gives a name through promise.
But the promise extends far beyond Abram himself.
It reaches all the families of the earth.
Genesis 12:3 is a Hinge of the Bible
Many theologians say Genesis 12:3 is one of the most important verses in the whole Bible.
Why? Because this is where God reveals that His plan of redemption will include all nations.
The rest of Scripture unfolds this promise.
From Abraham comes Israel.
 From Israel comes the Messiah.
 Through the Messiah the blessing reaches the nations.
Paul later explains this directly.
Galatians 3:8 says: “Scripture… preached the gospel beforehand to Abraham.”
Genesis 12 is the gospel in seed form.
~~ Quote ~~
Martin Luther: “This promise contains the whole gospel.”
For Luther, Genesis 12:3 already contained the future salvation of the world.
Illustration – The Acorn and the Oak
If you hold an acorn in your hand, it seems small.
But inside that acorn is the blueprint for a massive oak tree.
Roots spreading deep.
 Branches stretching wide.
Genesis 12:3 is like that acorn. The promise seems small.
One man. One promise.
But inside that promise is the salvation of the nations.
~~ APP ~~
Another preacher:
“The promise given to Abraham was always bigger than Abraham himself.”
God was not just blessing one man.
He was beginning a plan that would reach the entire world.

3. The Gospel Hidden in the Promise
Genesis 12 and Galatians 3
~~ EXEgesis ~~
Paul makes something remarkable clear in Galatians.
“The promises were made to Abraham and to his offspring… who is Christ.” (Galatians 3:16)
The ultimate fulfilment of Genesis 12 is not merely Israel.
It is Jesus Christ.
Through Christ the blessing promised to Abraham reaches the nations.
The gospel didn;t begin in the New Testament.
The gospel was already present in promise form.
~~ Quote ~~
Jonathan Edwards wrote:
“All the blessings of redemption are contained in the covenant with Abraham.”
Genesis 12 is the beginning of the unfolding plan of redemption.
~~ APP ~~
One preacher summarised the biblical storyline like this:
Abraham receives the promise.
 Israel carries the promise.
 Christ fulfils the promise.
 The nations receive the promise.
And today we are living inside the fulfilment of that promise.

5. Worship Before Fulfilment
Genesis 12:7–9
 7 Then the Lord appeared to Abram and said, “To your offspring I will give this land.” So he built there an altar to the Lord, who had appeared to him. 8 From there he moved to the hill country on the east of Bethel and pitched his tent, with Bethel on the west and Ai on the east. And there he built an altar to the Lord and called upon the name of the Lord. 9 And Abram journeyed on, still going toward the Negeb.
~~ EXEgesis ~~
Abram arrives in the land God promised.
Yet he still lives in tents.
He owns nothing.
And yet he builds an altar.
Twice the text tells us he worshipped.
Faith worships before the promise becomes visible.
~~ Quote ~~
John Owen: “Faith gives glory to God before the mercy is received.”
Abram honoured God while still waiting for fulfilment.
~~ APP ~~
Many people worship after the blessing arrives.
Faith worships while still waiting.
Abram trusted the promise enough to worship the God who gave it.

~~Conclusion~~
The Storyline of Redemption
9 After this I looked, and behold, a great multitude that no one could number, from every nation, from all tribes and peoples and languages, standing before the throne and before the Lamb, clothed in white robes, with palm branches in their hands,
From Promise to Fulfilment
Genesis 11 shows humanity trying to reach heaven.
Humanity gathers together and says,
“Let us make a name for ourselves.”
They build a tower.
But Genesis 12 shows God beginning a completely different plan.
Not a tower, A promise.
God speaks to one man and says,
“I will bless you… and in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed.”
sounds enormous.
How could one man become a blessing to the nations?
But as the story unfolds, we begin to see the answer.
From Abraham comes Israel.
From Israel comes the Messiah.
And through the Messiah the blessing reaches the world.
Paul  clearly in Galatians 3:16 “The promises were made to Abraham and to his offspring… who is Christ.”
Jesus is the fulfilment of the promise given in Genesis 12.

~~The Blessing Comes Through the Cross~~
Promise -> nations in a surprising way.
Not through political power.
Not through conquest.
But through the cross.
Paul says in Galatians 3:13–14:
“Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law by becoming a curse for us… so that in Christ Jesus the blessing of Abraham might come to the Gentiles.”
The blessing promised to Abraham comes through the death and resurrection of Christ.
Jesus takes the curse.
So the nations can receive the blessing.

~~The Nations Are Being Gathered~~
And today we are watching Genesis 12 unfold.
People from every tribe.
Every language.
Every nation.
Coming to Christ.
The promise given to Abraham is still being fulfilled.
The church itself is evidence that God kept His word.

~~The Personal Question~~
But the promise is not only global, It's personal. Galatians 3:29 says:
“If you are Christ’s, then you are Abraham’s offspring, heirs according to promise.”
The blessing promised to Abraham becomes yours through faith in Christ.
Not by ethnicity.
Not by heritage.
But by trusting the one who fulfilled the promise.
~~Final~~
Babel tried to reach heaven with a tower.
God reached the nations with a promise.
And that promise leads to Christ.
Abraham received the promise.
Christ fulfilled the promise.
And today the nations are receiving the promise.
The invitation is the same one Abraham faced.
Trust the God who made the promise.

The promise made to Abraham becomes the worship of the Lamb.
