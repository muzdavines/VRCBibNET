Easter 12/4/2026
Covenant and Command
Exodus 19-24 -  Theme: Law and grace pointing to Christ
Introduction - From Rescue to Relationship (inc. recap)
Israel were enslaved under Pharaoh
God heard, remembered, saw, and knew (Ex 2:24-25)
The plagues exposed false gods
The Passover lamb shielded from judgment
The Red Sea: salvation through judgment
KT:  God did not give the Law to save Israel
 He saved Israel first, then brought them to Himself: “I bore you on eagles’ wings and brought you to myself” (Ex 19:4)
¬ legal contract 1st: but redeemed people brought into covenant
As we move into Sinai, we need to see something clearly. The story of Israel isn’t abandoned later in the Bible; it's completed. What begins here at Sinai is going somewhere. The New Testament doesn’t throw this away; it shows us where it was always pointing.
Arc: Scattered in pride @ Babel, Now a people by grace

1. Grace Before Law - The Order Matters (V 19:3-6)
Scripture: Ex 19:4-6
~Exegesis~ “I bore you on eagles’ wings…”
 God speaks as the acting subject. Israel is carried, not cooperating.
“Now therefore…”  Obedience flows from rescue, not toward it.
“Treasure possession” (segullah) ¬ just owned, but personally valued, like a king’s private treasury.
Matt 13:45-46 ~ “Again, the kingdom of heaven is like a merchant in search of fine pearls, 46 who, on finding one pearl of great value, went and sold all that he had and bought it.
This is what Israel was called to be: a people who reflect God rightly in the world. And as the Old Testament unfolds, we see how difficult that calling actually is to live out.
~MetaP~ Sinai is ¬ a job interview, It is a family meeting after adoption
~Quote~
 “Men are never duly touched and impressed with a conviction of their insignificance, until they have contrasted themselves with the majesty of God.”
(ID comes from God, not self-establishment)
~Applic~
We instinctively reverse the order, We think: “If I obey, God will accept me”
But Exodus says: “I have already brought you out… now walk with me”
The story of Israel isn’t discarded in the New Testament, it’s fulfilled. The promises don’t disappear, they reach their goal.
PL: God does not rescue because we obey, We obey because He has rescued


2. The Holiness of God - Sinai Trembles (V 19:16-25)
Scripture: Ex 19:16-19
~Exegesis~ Thunder, lightning, trumpet, fire, ¬ decoration, but revelation
“The LORD descended” God must come down, man cannot climb
Boundaries enforced  Holiness is not symbolic, it is dangerous
~MetaP~: 
Mythology - Crete, Daedalus, Icarus 
standing too close to the sun The problem is not the sun’s anger It’s your inability to survive its purity
~Quote~ R. C. Sproul “God’s holiness is not simply the best we know infinitely better. It is utterly other.”
~Applic~
We don’t struggle to believe God is loving We struggle to grasp that He is holy
if we lose God’s holines, We lose the gospel
PL: Grace brings you near, Holiness reminds you why you couldn’t come

3. The Law Reveals God and Exposes Man (V 20:1-17)
Scripture: Ex 20
~Exegesis~ “I am the LORD your God who brought you out…”
So when Israel stands at Sinai and says, ‘All that the Lord has spoken we will do,’ the question quietly sits underneath the rest of the Bible…
Will they?  Law begins with redemption, not demand
Two tables: Godward (1-4), Manward (5-10)
The Law reflects God’s character, Not arbitrary rules, but revealed holiness
~MetaP~ The Law is not a ladder to climb, It’s a mirror you can’t argue with
~Quote~ Martin Luther
 “The law says, ‘Do this,’ and it is never done. Grace says, ‘Believe this,’ and everything is already done.”
~Applic~
Either: Lower the Law to fit ourselves, Or let it expose us
Jesus doesn’t relax the Law,  He deepens it to the heart (Jer 31:31-34)
“I will make a new covenant…”, “Not like the covenant that I made with their fathers…”, “I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts.”, “I will be their God, and they shall be my people.”, “They shall all know me…”, “For I will forgive their iniquity, and I will remember their sin no more.”
PL The Law doesn’t just show you what to do, It shows you who you really are


4. Fear and Distance - The Need for a Mediator (V 20:18-21)
Scripture: Ex 20:18-21
~Exegesis~ (flow)
“They stood far off”  Physical distance mirrors spiritual reality
“Moses drew near” The mediator goes where the people can’t
Moses steps into the gap for the people, but even Moses is not the final answer. The Bible is still moving toward a greater mediator.
“Do not let God speak…” They understand the danger correctly
~MetaP~
It’s like a courtroom where the evidence is overwhelming:
*  You don’t need more information
* You need someone to stand in your place
~Quote~
Charles Spurgeon:  “There is no meeting God except through a mediator.”
~Applic~
¬ need: More effort, Clarity , time
We need someone to bridge the gap
PL The problem ¬ distance in space - It is distance in holiness

5. Covenant Sealed in Blood V 24:3-8)
Scripture: Ex 24:7-8
~Exegesis~
The Law is read  The people agree
Then blood is applied
Half on the altar, half on the people This binds both parties
Blood = life (Lev 17:11)
This covenant is real, but it’s also fragile, because it depends on people who have already shown they cannot keep it.
Meaning:  Breaking covenant = death
“Behold the blood of the covenant” Directly echoed by Christ
~MetaP~
This is not ink on paper. It is life on the line
~Quote~
John Owen: “There is no death of sin without the death of Christ.”
~Applic~
Even at Sinai
 Law alone is not enough
Blood is required
PL At Sinai, blood maintains the covenant
 At the cross, blood fulfils it

6. A Meal in God’s Presence - Grace Breaking Through V 24:9-11)
Scripture: Ex 24:9-11
~Exegesis~
“They saw the God of Israel” ~ A real but mediated vision
“He did not lay his hand on them” ~ They should die, but they don’t
“They ate and drank” ~ Covenant fellowship, peace, acceptance
~MetaP~
It’s like being invited to sit at the King’s table,  After expecting execution
~Quote~
   Augustine: “You have made us for Yourself, and our heart is restless until it rests in You.”
~Applic~ : God doesn’t only command, He invites But the invitation rests on sacrifice
PL: The God who descended in fire -Now sits with His people at a table

Conc- Law and Grace Meet at the Cross
Sinai leaves unresolved tension:
God is holy,  The Law is good, The people are not
~Metap~ 
Sinai is a mountain you cannot climb. The cross is God coming down to carry you
~Quote~ John Stott: “The essence of sin is man substituting himself for God; the essence of salvation is God substituting Himself for man.”

~Christ fulfilment~
* True Israel ~ Matt 2:15, Gal 3:16
* Greater Moses ~ Deut 18:15, Acts 3:22–23
* Perfect law-keeper ~ Matt 5:17, Heb 4:15
* Final sacrifice ~ Heb 10:10–14
* Better mediator ~ 1 Tim 2:5, Heb 8:6
Jeremiah fulfilled: Law written not on stone, but on hearts

Final Preaching Lines
Don’t stand at a distance like Israel, try to climb Sinai
Come to Christ Because: Abel’s blood cries for justice,  Christ’s blood speaks mercy
The mountain burned with fire,  But the cross stands open
And the God who said, “Do not come near” Now says in Christ, “Come.”
