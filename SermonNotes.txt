From Slavery to Salvation
Scripture: Exodus 1–15
 Focus: The Exodus as a Type of Redemption
Introduction – From Promise to Chains
Before exodus – recap
Abraham, Isaac
Then  Jacob, transformed at Peniel.
Jacob -> Israel -> Family.
By the end of Genesis, that family has grown into a people… not in the land.
They are in Egypt.
Joseph, the rejected brother, becomes the instrument of preservation.
 Through suffering, God saves His people from famine.
So Israel goes down into Egypt…70 persons.
Safe. Provided for. Protected.
Exodus opens with a shift: “Now there arose a new king over Egypt, who did not know Joseph.” (Exodus 1:8)
What began as refuge becomes bondage.
And that is the pattern of sin.
What promises life… eventually enslaves.

1. Slavery Under Pharaoh
Scripture: Exodus 1:8–14
“They set taskmasters over them… they made their lives bitter with hard bondage.”
8 Now there arose a new king over Egypt, who did not know Joseph. 9 And he said to his people, “Behold, the people of Israel are too many and too mighty for us. 10 Come, let us deal shrewdly with them, lest they multiply, and, if war breaks out, they join our enemies and fight against us and escape from the land.” 11 Therefore they set _TASKMASTERS_ over them to _AFFLICT_  them with heavy burdens. They built for Pharaoh store cities, Pithom and Raamses. 12 But the more they were oppressed, the more they multiplied and the more they spread abroad. And the Egyptians were in dread of the people of Israel. 13 So they ruthlessly made the people of Israel work as slaves 14 and made their lives _BITTER_ with hard service_BONDAGE/SLAVERY_, in mortar and brick, and in all kinds of work in the field. In all their work they RUTHLESS  made them work as SLAVES.
~Exegesis~
Israel multiplies. God is keeping His promise.
But Pharaoh sees blessing as threat.
So he enslaves them.
The language: “taskmasters” “afflicted” “bitter” “bondage/slavery”
 Inconvenient?  This is total domination.
Israel cannot free themselves.
~John Calvin~ “The more the church is oppressed, the more it grows, for God’s power is then more clearly seen.”
IRan
~Application~
This is not just Israel’s story.
This is our story.
Sin begins as something we choose, It ends as something that rules us.
 “Whoever commits sin is a slave of sin.” (John 8:34)
Inconvenit? Not struggling. managing.
Slave. No negotiation.
 You don’t improve your chains.
You need rescue.


2. The Cry of the Oppressed
Scripture: Exodus 2:23–25
“The children of Israel groaned… and their cry came up to God.”
23 During those many days the king of Egypt died, and the people of Israel groaned because of their slavery and cried out for help. Their cry for rescue from slavery came up to God. 24 And God _HEARD_ their groaning, and God _REMEMBERED_ his covenant with Abraham, with Isaac, and with Jacob. 25 God _SAW_ the people of Israel—and God _KNEW_.
~Exegesis~
They cry, They don’t strategise, They don’t revolt successfully.
Text: God heard, remembered, saw, knew
Personal imminent, Caring Covenant language.
God is about to act *because of His promise*, not their performance.
~Augustine~ “He who made us without us will not save us without Himself.”
~Application~
Salvation strength.
It begins when you realise you have none.
Some people never come to Christ because they are still trying to manage Egypt.
But redemption begins when the cry goes up:
“I cannot save myself.”

3. The God Who Comes Down
Scripture: Exodus 3:7–8
“I have surely seen… I have heard… I know… and I have come down to deliver them.”
7 Then the Lord said, “I have surely seen the affliction of my people who are in Egypt and have heard their cry because of their taskmasters. I know their sufferings, 8 and I have come down to deliver them out of the hand of the Egyptians and to bring them up out of that land to a good and broad land, a land flowing with milk and honey, to the place of the Canaanites, the Hittites, the Amorites, the Perizzites, the Hivites, and the Jebusites. 
~Exegesis~
This is the turning point.
God does not send advice.He comes down.
 Genesis 11:At Babel, man tries to go up. Here, God comes down.
Direction!!!! 
~John Owen~
 “All saving acts begin in the heart of God, not in the will of man.”
~Application~
Not us climbing / God descending.
And this points forward directly to Christ:
“The Word became flesh and dwelt among us.” (John 1:14)
Palm Sunday reminds us:
He came down… not just into the world, but toward the cross.

4. The Passover Lamb
Scripture: Exodus 12:5–7, 12–13
“Your lamb shall be without blemish… and the blood shall be a sign for you… when I see the blood, I will pass over you.”
5 Your lamb shall be without blemish, a male a year old. You may take it from the sheep or from the goats, 6 and you shall keep it until the fourteenth day of this month, when the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall kill their lambs at twilight. 7 “Then they shall take some of the blood and put it on the two doorposts and the lintel of the houses in which they eat it.
12 For I will pass through the land of Egypt that night, and I will strike all the firstborn in the land of Egypt, both man and beast; and on all the gods of Egypt I will execute judgments: I am the Lord. 13 The blood shall be a sign for you, on the houses where you are. And when I see the blood, I will pass over you, and no plague will befall you to destroy you, when I strike the land of Egypt.
~Exegesis~
Judgment is coming.
Not just on Egypt… on all.
Every house will face death.
?¬: “Are you Israelite or Egyptian?”
?=Is there blood?
The lamb dies in place of the firstborn.
Substitution.
~Charles Spurgeon~
 “It is not your hold of Christ that saves you, it is Christ. It is not your joy in Christ, it is Christ.”
~Application~
Type of Christ: “Christ, our Passover, was sacrificed for us.” (1 Corinthians 5:7)
The blood is not symbolic sentiment.
It is judicial protection.
God does not say: “When I see your effort…”
He says: “When I see the blood.”
Palm Sunday leads here.
The Lamb enters Jerusalem… to become the Passover.
