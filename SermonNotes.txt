From Sinai to Pentecost
The Holy God Dwelling with His People
Acts 2:1-13
Introduction
Throughout Scripture, there is a question that keeps surfacing:
How can sinful people live in the presence of a holy God?
It is one of the great themes of the Bible.
In Eden, Adam and Eve are driven from God's presence because of sin.
In Exodus, Israel is redeemed from Egypt, but when God descends upon Mount Sinai, boundaries are erected around the mountain. The people are warned not to come near.

In the tabernacle and temple, curtains separate people from the Holy of Holies.

Again and again the same reality confronts us: 
      . God desires to dwell with His people.
      . Yet sin creates separation.
      . Then we come to Pentecost.

At first glance it seems dramatic and unusual.
Wind, Fir, Tongues, Different languages, Crowds gathering in amazement.


But beneath all those visible signs lies something far more profound.
Pentecost answers the question that has echoed throughout Scripture.
The God who descended upon Sinai now comes to dwell within His people.
Not because His holiness has diminished or because sin no longer matters.
But because Christ has accomplished what Sinai never could.
Today we are going to trace that movement.
      .From Sinai to Pentecost.
      .From distance to access.
      .From stone tablets to transformed hearts.
      .From a mountain filled with fire to a people filled with the Spirit.
1. The Day God Came Down Again
Acts 2:1-4 : "When the day of Pentecost arrived, they were all together in one place."
Pentecost was not originally a Christian celebration.
It was one of Israel's great feast days: Fifty days after Passover, pilgrims from across the Roman world gathered in Jerusalem to celebrate the Feast of Weeks.
The city would have been crowded, busy, visitors.

For the disciples it had also been a season of waiting.
      . Jesus had died.
      . Jesus had risen.
      . Jesus had ascended.
And before ascending He had given a promise:
       "You will be baptized with the Holy Spirit not many days from now." (Acts 1:5)
So they waited not knowing exactly what would happen, Simply Obeying

Then suddenly:
         "There came from heaven a sound like a mighty rushing wind."
Notice where it comes from, Not from earth Not from the disciples. Not from human effort.

From heaven. 
The initiative belongs entirely to God. Pentecost is not humanity reaching upward. It is God coming downward. - Babel inverted
Then Luke describes:
      "Divided tongues as of fire appeared to them and rested on each one of them."
Immediately, every Jewish reader should think of Sinai.
When God descended upon the mountain:
     "Mount Sinai was wrapped in smoke because the LORD had descended on it in fire." (Exodus 19:18)
The imagery is unmistakable.
Fire,  Divine presence, God drawing near.
The God of Sinai has arrived.

But something extraordinary has changed.
      . At Sinai the fire rested on a mountain.
      .At Pentecost the fire rests upon people.
      .At Sinai God descended to a location.
      .At Pentecost God descends to indwell believers.
The presence once confined to sacred space is now given to God's people.

Historical Insight
Throughout the Old Testament. the Spirit occasionally came upon individuals.
Moses, Joshua,  David, The prophets.
But now the Spirit is poured out upon the whole covenant community.
What Joel prophesied centuries earlier has begun:
               "I will pour out my Spirit on all flesh."
The age of fulfilment has arrived.
John Calvin wrote "The visible signs were added that believers might know the Spirit had truly been given."
The wind and fire are signs, They are not the main event.
The main event is God's presence dwelling among His people.
Illustration
Imagine being invited into Buckingham Palace,
That would be remarkable enough.
But imagine the King then saying: "I am coming to live in your house."
That is closer to what Pentecost represents.
Not merely access to God's presence. 
God's presence taking up residence within His people.
~Application~
Many Christians unintentionally live as if Pentecost never happened.
They view Christianity primarily as rules.Duties, Obligations, Moral improvement.
But Christianity is fundamentally supernatural.
The Christian life is impossible apart from the Holy Spirit.
     . You cannot manufacture holiness.
     . You cannot generate spiritual life.
     .. You cannot transform your own heart.
Christianity begins not with human effort but with divine presence.
God comes to us before we can ever come to Him.
2. The Problem Sinai Revealed
Exodus 19
To appreciate Pentecost we must revisit Sinai.
Israel has been delivered from Egypt,  The Red Sea, Pharaoh's armies.
God has redeemed His people.Then they arrive at Mount Sinai.
And God descends. Terrifying. Thunder , Lightning, Smoke covers the mountain, Trumpets blast.The earth trembles, The entire mountain shakes violently.
It is one of the most awe-inspiring scenes in all of Scripture.
Yet alongside all the majesty runs another theme.

Distance.
Again and again God warns:  Stay back, don’t cross the boundary, touch the mountain
Why? Because God is holy.
¬ that God is unloving, but utterly pure.
His holiness is overwhelming. And sinful people cannot casually enter His presence.

~Exegesis~
Exodus 19 is preparing Israel to understand the seriousness of God's holiness.
Modern culture often speaks about God as though He were simply a larger version of ourselves.
Sinai destroys that illusion.
God is not merely greater than us.
He is categorically different from us: Perfectly
      . holy.
      . righteous.
      . pure.

R. C. Sproul "When we understand something of the holiness of God, we understand something of the seriousness of sin."

That is Sinai's lesson.
The Failure of the Law
The Law that follows is good, Holy, Perfect.
The problem is not the Law, The problem is the human heart.
Within weeks Israel is worshipping a golden calf.
The commandments expose sin, can’t cure it
The Law can tell us what righteousness looks like. It cannot make us righteous.
~Metaphor~
A mirror reveals dirt,  It can show every stain and blemish.
But no one washes their face with a mirror.
The mirror diagnoses the problem It cannot solve it.

The Law functions similarly.
It reveals our condition.
It cannot transform our hearts.
~Application~
Many people still approach God as if moral effort were enough.
"I try to be a good person." "I do my best." "I keep most of the rules."
Sinai confronts that thinking.
The standard is not relative goodness but God's holiness.
And none of us meets that standard, 
The problem exposed at Sinai remains humanity's greatest problem today.
We need more than instruction, We need transformation.
3. What Changed Between Sinai and Pentecost?
This raises an obvious question.
If God was too holy for sinners to approach at Sinai, how can His Spirit now dwell within believers?
What changed?
The answer is Christ.
Between Sinai and Pentecost stands the greatest event in human history.
The cross.
Jesus came as the true obedient Son.
      . Where Israel failed, He succeeded.
      . Where Adam rebelled, He obeyed.
      .Where we have sinned, He remained perfect.
      . Then at Calvary He bore the curse of the broken Law. The judgment our sin deserved fell upon Him.
The wrath that should have fallen upon us was poured out upon Christ.
And after dying for sinners He rose again.
      . Victorious over sin.
      . Victorious over death.
      .Victorious over the grave.
Then He ascended to the Father's right hand.

Peter makes exactly this point later in Acts 2: 33
    "Being therefore exalted at the right hand of God, and having received from the Father the promise of the Holy Spirit, he has poured out this."
Pentecost is not primarily about the disciples, It is about the enthroned Christ.
The Spirit comes because Jesus reigns.
Quote
John Owen: "All the treasures of grace are communicated to us by the Holy Spirit from the fullness of Christ."
The Spirit's coming is evidence that Christ's work is complete.

~Application~
Every blessing we enjoy as Christians flows through Christ.
Forgiveness, Adoption, Justification, Sanctification, The gift of the Spirit Himself.
Pentecost reminds us that Christianity is not built upon our efforts to reach God.
It rests entirely upon Christ's finished work.
4. The Law Written on Hearts
Centuries before Pentecost God had promised something astonishing.
Through Jeremiah He declared:
     "I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts." V31:33
Through Ezekiel He promised:
     "I will give you a new heart... and I will put my Spirit within you." 36:26-27
Notice the difference.
Sinai wrote God's law on stone. The New Covenant writes God's law on hearts.
The Spirit does internally what the Law could never accomplish externally.
      . He changes desires.
      . He renews affections.
      . He convicts of sin.
      . He produces holiness.
      . He makes us increasingly like Christ.
Illustration
You can force a tree to stand upright by tying it to a stake.
But eventually the rope comes off. True change happens when life flows from within.
That is the Spirit's work. Not mere behaviour modification.Heart transformation.
~Application~
Many believers become discouraged because they still see remaining sin.
Pentecost does not mean instant perfection.
The Christian life remains a battle. But the Spirit is actively working within us.
The desires have changed, The direction has changed.
The heart has changed.
What God commands He now empowers.
5. From Sinai to Zion
Hebrews 12:18-24
The writer to the Hebrews brings everything together.
He deliberately contrasts two mountains.
First Sinai: The mountain of fire, Darkness, Fear, Judgment.
Then Zion: The heavenly Jerusalem, The city of God, The gathering of the redeemed, The presence of Christ.
Listen!: "You have not come to what may be touched... but you have come to Mount Zion."
Tense: Not "you will come." "You have come."
Because of Christ believers already possess access to God.
The barriers have fallen. The veil has been torn.The way is open.
~Exegesis~
Hebrews does not suggest God has become less holy. Quite the opposite.
The same holy God remains,  what has changed is our standing before Him.
Christ's righteousness has become ours,  Therefore we approach boldly.
Not because we are worthy, Because Christ is worthy.
~Application~
Many Christians still live as though they remain at Sinai.
      . Always fearful.
      . Always uncertain.
      .Always wondering whether God accepts them.

Hebrews says look to Christ.
     . The Judge has become your Father.
     . The Holy One has welcomed you through the Son.
     . The Spirit dwelling within you is proof that you belong to Him.
Conclusion
At Sinai:
      . Fire rested on a mountain.
      . Boundaries kept people away.
      . The Law was written on stone.
      . Holiness exposed sin.

At Pentecost:
      . Fire rested on God's people.
      . Access was opened through Christ.
      . The Law was written on hearts.
      . The Spirit transformed lives.
The God who descended at Sinai is the same God who descended at Pentecost.
His holiness has not changed, glory has not diminished, righteousness has not weakened.
What changed was the work of Christ.

Because Christ lived the life we could not live.
Because Christ died the death we deserved.
Because Christ rose victorious.
Because Christ ascended in triumph.


The Holy Spirit has been poured out upon His people.
The fire that once warned sinners away now rests upon those who belong to Jesus.
And one day the story will reach its final fulfilment.

No mountain, veil, temple, separation.
Only God's people dwelling forever in God's presence through the Lamb.
Until then we live as Pentecost people.
Indwelt by the Spirit. Empowered by grace. Walking in holiness. Proclaiming Christ.
And waiting for the day when faith becomes sight. Amen.
