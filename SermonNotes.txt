Now that we have read through the Passion of Christ, what exactly is it? /It is/ the most heart wrenching part of the Bible, and the reason for that isn’t just what happens to Jesus, but what exactly He represents in that situation on a prophetic level. You heard the Passion Narrative, that Jesus was betrayed into the custody of the sanhedrin, accused of blasphemy, mocked, beaten, condemned to death by crucifixion, and humiliated in front of multitudes of people over the course of the entire ordeal, but there’s a little more to it than just that. Jesus is referred to as The Lamb many times, notably when He and John the Baptist meet in chapter 1 where John says, “Behold, the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world!” This is in reference to the sin offering required by Levitical law for the atonement of sins. Without atonement, the cost of disobeying one of God’s commandments was the life of the perpetrator; As mentioned in Genesis 2, “... The Lord God commanded (Adam) the man, saying, “You may surely eat of every tree of the garden, but of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil you shall not eat, for in the day that you eat of it you shall surely die.” God provided a path for atonement to the Israelites through the substitution of their own life with the life of a ceremonially appropriate animal, which would be presented to a priest at the temple to be slain on behalf of the perpetrator. This sacrifice saved the sinner from the consequences of their disobedience, but it did not remove their sin, it only covered it, since the value of an animal’s life is not sufficient to fully redeem a human one. Jesus, in demonstrating Himself to be the Messiah through His annunciation, miracles, and sinless life, also demonstrated Himself to be the ultimate final sacrifice as foretold by the prophets; An infinite God whose life is of incomprehensible value compared to a human’s, substituting His own life in punishment for the lives of all humankind. In order to accomplish this ultimate sacrifice, Jesus would have to follow in the footsteps of a sin offering, as prescribed by the law in order to fulfill it in every regard. He went into Jerusalem knowing that He would be delivered by a sinner before the priests at the temple to be sentenced to death; A mirror image of an unblemished sacrificial lamb, being walked into the temple by a perpetrator of God’s law to be slain for the atonement of their sins. Jesus did all this under His own volition, and accepted His fate with complete submission. Despite all the false testimony being leveled against Him, the assault that He sustained before being formally sentenced, and the scourging He received afterward, He never put up a defense for himself, fulfilling the prophecy in Isaiah 53, “He was oppressed, and he was afflicted, yet he opened not his mouth; like a lamb that is led to the slaughter, and like a sheep that before its shearers is silent, so he opened not his mouth.” While the Passion of Christ is a somber culmination of all the law and prophets, we have reason to rejoice, that through Christ’s fulfillment of those laws and prophecies, complete forgiveness for sin has been made accessible to the whole of humanity, and does not require any ceremonial rituals or shedding of blood to receive.
