Atonement for Sin
Leviticus 16
 The Day of Atonement and Christ our High Priest

Intro
We’ve come out of Egypt.- Redemption - blood, brought out
The Law.  God has spoken. defined how He is to be worshipped.
Still a problem: God now dwells in the camp,  sin still dwells in the people.
?? How can a holy God remain among a sinful people without consuming them?
Leviticus 16 answers that question. Not with theory.h blood.
READ
1. Access Is Restricted
Leviticus 16:1–2
“The LORD spoke… after the death of the two sons of Aaron…” scrifice , irreverence
That moment hangs over this chapter.
Nadab and Abihu approached God wrongly. And they died.
So God says:“Tell Aaron… not to come at any time… lest he die.”
This is about access.God is not absent. But He is not casually accessible.
Not even the high priest walks in freely.
Once a year.  With blood. Under command.
Quote
 John Calvin: “Man’s nature is a perpetual factory of idols.”
Left to ourselves, we don’t approach God rightly, We reshape Him.
 We come on our terms.
Leviticus says: you cannot do that and live.
Metaphor
 It’s like entering a high-voltage room. The danger is real. The protocol is not optional.
Application
 We still do this today.
“I think God is like this…”  “I don’t see why that matters…”
But access to God is not self-defined.
It is God-defined.
And if there is to be access, it must come through atonement.

2. The Mediator Is Not Enough
Leviticus 16:3–6
Aaron must prepare.
He must wash.  He must wear specific garments.  He must offer a sacrifice for his own sin.
Before he represents the people, he must be cleansed himself.
The mediator is not perfect.
Quote
 Charles Spurgeon: “The priest himself needed a sacrifice, and so proved that he was not sufficient.”
Metaphor
 Like a bridge that cannot hold its own weight,  it cannot carry others across.
Application
 Every human mediator fails.
Priests fail.
 Leaders fail.
 We fail.
Which means this system is pointing beyond itself.
We don’t just need a priest.
We need a perfect High Priest.

3. Two Goats: Judgment and Removal
Leviticus 16:7–10
Two goats are brought forward.
Lots are cast. One for the LORD. One for Azazel.
We’re not told exactly what Azazel is, and that’s not the point. One goat dies, the other carries sin away. Judgment and removal. That’s what matters, and that’s what points to Christ.
The first goat is sacrificed.
Its blood is taken into the Most Holy Place.
Sin is not ignored,  It is judged.
Metaphor
 The mercy seat becomes the place where justice and mercy meet.
Then the second goat.
Hands are laid on it. Sins are confessed over it.
And it is sent away.
Carrying sin into the wilderness.
Quote
 John Stott: “We have not only been forgiven; we have been released.”
Metaphor
 One goat dies under the weight. The other disappears with it.
Application
 We don’t just need sin forgiven.
We need sin removed.
Guilt dealt with,  Burden lifted.
4. Blood Before God: Sin Must Be Dealt With
Leviticus 16:11–19
The high priest enters the Most Holy Place.
Not without blood. He sprinkles it on the mercy seat. Because sin has defiled everything. Even the place where God dwells.
Quote
 Leon Morris: “The essence of sin is man substituting himself for God, while the essence of salvation is God substituting Himself for man.”
Metaphor
 Sin ¬ a surface issue.  It spreads. It settles. It stains.
Application:  We minimise sin, call it weakness, call it mistake.
But Scripture treats it as something that must be atoned for / made right.
¬  managed or explained away. Has to be Dealt with.

5. The Scapegoat: Gone Means Gone
Leviticus 16:20–22
All the sins of the people are confessed, on the goat,  led away to wilderness, neverr to return.
Quote
 J.I. Packer: “God’s forgiveness is not merely the cancelling of debt, but the removal of the offender from the charge.”
Metaphor:  Watch it walk, further & further until it disappears.
Application
 Many people believe in forgiveness, still carry guilt, rehearse failure.
If God has removed it,  why are you carrying it?

6. Repeated Atonement: Not Yet Finished
Leviticus 16:29–34
“This shall be a statute forever…”
Year after year: again & again…
Quote  Heb 10:1: “The law has but a shadow of the good things to come…”
Metaphor:  Like resetting a warning light, not fixing the engine.
Application:  This system holds judgment back, it doesnt remove sin permanently. Which means it is pointing forward, greater is coming.
7. Christ Our High Priest
This is where everything lands. Leviticus 16 is not the destination.
It is the shadow, Christ is the reality ~ 
A better priest. : Sinless. Perfect.  Eternal. A better sacrifice, He does not bring blood, He is the sacrifice.
Quote ~  John Owen: “What the law could not do, Christ has done once for all.”
A once-for-all work, Not repeated, Finished. “It is finished.”!!!
And He fulfils both goats.under judgment, carries sin away.
Metaphor;  At the cross, nothing is left undone. No remainder. No residue.
Conclusion
Leviticus 16 shows us: Sin is serious,  Access is costly,  Atonement is necessary.
But it also shows us its own limitation, cant finish what it begins.
Only Christ can. And He has.

Final Application
So now the question is not abstract, It is personal.
Are you still trying to approach God your own way? Still trying to carry your sin? Still trying to atone for yourself?
Or have you come through the only way God has provided? ¬ ritual but own effort. Through Christ.
Put the weight down! The sin has been carried away.
The blood has been shed.
Come to the High Priest  who does not keep you at a distance,  but brings you near.
