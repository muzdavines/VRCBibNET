Into the Land
Joshua 1–6
Joshua (Yeshua) as a Type of Christ
Intro

Last time we stood at the edge of the wilderness.

God had redeemed His people from Egypt.
      .He had brought them through the sea.
      .He had given them the law.
      .He had dwelt among them in the tabernacle.
      .He had provided sacrifice and atonement.

But redemption was never meant to end in wandering.

God ¬ rescue Israel JUST to bring them out of slavery.
He brought them OUT so He could bring them IN.

!!!! There’s a Thud!.
> “After the death of Moses the servant of the LORD…” v1:1

The great prophet. -> The mediator of the covenant, he stood before Pharaoh, he ascended Sinai. – GONE.

Enter Joshua - now Joshua stands at the Jordan.

Israel is no longer looking backward toward Egypt. But looking forward toward inheritance.

And Joshua matters deeply in the story of redemption.

Hebrew = Yehoshua, often shortened   to Yeshua.
“The LORD saves.”  Greek form Jesus. ¬ accidental.

Joshua becomes a type, a shadow, a pointer forward to Christ.

Moses brings the people to the edge.
Joshua brings them into the land.

The law can guide, expose, instruct, and condemn.
But only the Lord who saves can bring His people into rest.

And that theme will run through the entire sermon.

Joshua is not merely a military hero.He is a signpost pointing forward to the greater Joshua, Jesus Christ.


---

1. The Leader Who Brings God’s People In
Joshua 1:1–9 > “Moses my servant is dead. Now therefore arise, go over this Jordan…”
There is both grief and movement here.

God acknowledges Moses faithfully: > “Moses my servant…”

But the mission continues. God’s purposes are never buried with one man. Joshua is commissioned.

Notice how often courage appears in this section.
> “Be strong and courageous.” Repeated again and again.
Why? Because Joshua is standing in enormous shoes.

Moses is one of the towering figures of Scripture. - big shoes
Yet God does not tell Joshua to imitate Moses’ personality.

He tells him: > “I will be with you.” the key.
The confidence of Joshua is not Joshua himself. It is the presence of God.
3: > “Every place that the sole of your foot will tread upon I have given to you.”
Certainty.  “I might give.” or “We’ll see how well you perform.” “I have given.”

The promise precedes the conquest.Inheritance rests first upon God’s covenant faithfulness.

And yet Israel must still walk forward in obedience. This is covenantal faith in action.
~Christ Connection~
This is where Joshua begins pointing us toward Christ.

Moses can’t bring Israel into the inheritance. Joshua does.

That is deeply symbolic. 

The law cannot save.
    . The law reveals sin.
    . The law exposes weakness.
    . The law shows God’s holiness.

But the law cannot carry sinners into rest. Only Jesus.

Hebrews later develops this explicitly.

Joshua gave Israel a form of rest in the land, but not ultimate rest.

Because the true and final rest is found in Christ Himself.

~METAPHOR~
Moses brings the people to the doorway.
Joshua opens the door and leads them through.

Quote

Spurgeon> “God never calls His people forward without also going before them.” 
Canaan was never the final answer to human restlessness. CHRIST IS.
~APPLICATION~
Many people live permanently at the border.
CLOSE TO  truth, church, Scripture. - have the Notitia, Assensus, can’t get to Fiducia
But never entering fully into trust and surrender.
God DID NOT  redeem His people for endless wandering.

And spiritually, many believers still live like wilderness people when Christ has opened the way into deeper fellowship, deeper trust, and deeper obedience.

---
2. Through the Waters
Joshua 3:7–17

Now Israel reaches the Jordan River.

And the text makes a point of telling us: > “The Jordan overflows all its banks throughout the time of harvest.”

¬ a shallow stream, it's  impossible territory.
And then comes God’s instruction.
The priests carrying the Ark must step into the water first.
Ark Central - The Ark represents the presence of God among His people.
So -  God goes first.

The priests step forward, and when their feet touch the water: the river stops.

Israel crosses on dry ground - echoes of the Red Sea.

Progression.
    . At the Red Sea: God brought Israel out of slavery.
    . At the Jordan: God brings them into inheritance.
    .This is movement in redemption history.
~Christ Connection~
Christ also goes before His people: suffering, judgement, death itself - the waters first.
And because He goes before us, His people pass safely through.

~METAPHOR~   Israel doesn’t  build a bridge over the Jordan, God opens a path through impossibility.

John Owen > “Christ is the way whereby we pass safely through all dangers unto eternal rest.”
~APPLICATION~

Faith often requires stepping forward before the waters move.
Difficult - We want guarantees first.
But biblical faith trusts the character of God before it sees the outcome.
Israel had to place weight upon God’s promise.
So do we.

3. Stones of Remembrance
Joshua 4:1–24

God commands twelve stones to be taken from the riverbed.

Why? Memory.

V6: > “When your children ask in time to come, ‘What do those stones mean to you?’”

God builds remembrance into the life of His people.Because human beings forget. n QUICKLY!

Israel is not to treat the Jordan crossing as random history.

It must become covenant memory.

The next generation must hear the story.

~METAPHOR~ The stones are sermons without words.

~APPLICATION~

The church also lives by remembrance.
     . Baptism remembers union with Christ.
     . Communion remembers the cross.
     . Preaching remembers God’s mighty acts.
     . The gospel is NOT vague spirituality.  It is rooted in real events in history.

Christ truly died. Christ truly rose. Christ truly saves.
A church that forgets becomes spiritually fragile.
Martin Luther> “We need to hear the gospel every day because we forget it every day.”
That is exactly why God gives memorials.

4. The Commander of the Lord’s Army
Joshua 5:13–15

This is one of the most striking moments in Joshua.

Joshua sees a man standing before him with a drawn sword.

Joshua asks:

> “Are you for us, or for our adversaries?”
astonishing: > “No.”

Joshua asked the wrong question.
The issue is not: “Is God on my side?” The issue is: “Am I submitted to Him?”

The figure identifies Himself: > “I am the commander of the army of the LORD.”
Joshua immediately falls facedown.

And then comes familiar language:
> “Take off your sandals from your feet, for the place where you are standing is holy.”

Sound familiar ? That echoes Moses at the burning bush.

Many believe this is a theophany, possibly even a pre-incarnate appearance of Christ.
Whether one presses that fully or not, the scene is unmistakably holy.

Joshua may lead Israel, but Joshua himself is under authority.

~Christ Connection~
Joshua is ¬ the ultimate commander. Christ is.

Jesus is not simply our assistant in achieving our plans.He is KING, LORD, COMMANDER.

Joshua enters Canaan as commander, then immediately discovers someone higher-ranking already stands there.

~APPLICATION~

Modern Christianity often tries to recruit Jesus into our agenda.
      . Politics.
      . Identity.
      . Personal ambition.

But discipleship begins with surrender.

Not: “Lord, bless my plans.” But: “Lord, command my life.”

5. The Walls Fall by Faith
Joshua 6:1–21

Jericho is shut tightly. Humanly speaking, it is secure.

God gives Israel an utterly strange strategy.
      . March.
      . Wait.
      . Blow trumpets.
      . Remain silent.
      . Walk again.
From a military perspective, it looks absurd.Why?

Because God is teaching Israel from the beginning that victory belongs to Him.
The conquest will not become a monument to human strength.

Hebrews 11 later explains:
> “By faith the walls of Jericho fell down…”
Not by engineering, explosives, or military genius.
By faith.

~Christ Connection~
God often conquers through what appears weak.
The cross looked weak. A crucified Messiah looked foolish.

Yet through apparent weakness God triumphed over sin, death, and hell.

~METAPHOR~

Jericho collapses because God’s word is stronger than stone walls.

Bonhoeffer wrote: > “The victory is won before the battle begins.”

~APPLICATION~
Faith obeys even when it does not fully understand the strategy.
There are moments where God’s commands seem strange to the world.
     .Forgiveness, Humility, Holiness,Patience.

But God’s wisdom is not measured by human expectations.

6. Rahab and the Scarlet Cord
Joshua 2 and 6:22–25

Rahab is one of the most beautiful pictures of grace in the Old Testament.

She is: a Canaanite, prostitute, outsider, living in a condemned city

Yet she hears about the God of Israel and believes.
      . Faith begins with hearing.
      . She hides the spies.
      . She asks for mercy.
      . And the scarlet cord marks her house.

Judgement falls on Jericho. Rahab is spared.
And not only spared, She is brought into Israel.

Even more astonishing: she becomes part of the genealogy of Christ.

~Christ Connection~

Rahab reminds us salvation is by grace through faith.
Not heritage, Not morality, Not background. — MERCY

~METAPHOR~
In a city under judgement, one window hangs out the colour of mercy.

~APPLICATION~

Nobody is beyond the reach of grace.
Some people assume they are too broken, too stained, too far gone.
Rahab says otherwise.
Christ delights in rescuing outsiders.

Conclusion
Greater Joshua, Greater Rest
Joshua brings Israel into the land. But Joshua is not the end of the story.

Because Canaan itself was never the final destination.
Sin still existed.Death still existed.

The human heart still needed redemption.

Heb 4:8 > “If Joshua had given them rest, God would not have spoken of another day later on.”

Joshua points beyond himself. To Jesus, The greater Yeshua.

The true Lord who saves.

Joshua crossed the Jordan into earthly inheritance.
Jesus crossed through death into resurrection life.

Joshua led Israel against Jericho.
Jesus defeats sin, Satan, and death itself.

Joshua rescued Rahab from earthly judgement.
Jesus rescues sinners from eternal judgement.

Joshua gave temporary rest in a land.
Jesus gives eternal rest in Himself.

And Revelation shows the final inheritance: a gathered people from every tribe and tongue dwelling forever with God.

The wilderness ends. The journey ends.
The greater Joshua brings His people home.

 ~APPLICATION~
The call is not: “Be brave like Joshua.”
The deeper call is: Trust the greater Joshua.

Follow Him.

Because He does not merely improve lives.

He brings dead sinners into eternal inheritance.

He is the Lord who saves.
